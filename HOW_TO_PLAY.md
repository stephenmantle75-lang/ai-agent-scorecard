# How to Play

## The basics

1. Enter any GitHub **username** or **repo URL** in the input box
2. Hit **Generate Score**
3. Get your scorecard — tier, archetype, metrics, and signals
4. Your score is automatically saved to the leaderboard
5. Copy your share link and post it

---

## The six metrics

Your total score is out of **100 points** across six dimensions:

### Consistency (25 pts)
How often you commit. Measured over the last 28 days.
- 25 pts → 150+ commits
- 15 pts → 50+ commits
- 5 pts → fewer than 10 commits

**How to improve:** Commit small, commit often. Daily commits beat weekly dumps.

---

### Diversity (20 pts)
How many languages and domains you work across.
- Covers: AI/ML, DevOps, Frontend, Backend, Security, Data, Automation, Finance, Testing, Infrastructure
- More domains = higher score

**How to improve:** Push projects in languages you don't normally use. Even small experiments count.

---

### Quality (20 pts)
How well your repos are documented.
- READMEs on repos
- Project descriptions
- Licence files

**How to improve:** Add a README to every repo, even one-liners. Fill in the Description field in GitHub repo settings.

---

### Impact (20 pts)
Your reach: stars, forks, and watchers across all public repos.
- 20 pts → 500+ total stars
- 10 pts → 50+ stars
- 2 pts → under 10 stars

**How to improve:** Build things people want. Share your repos on relevant communities.

---

### Learning (10 pts)
Growth signal — new repos created recently, especially in new languages.

**How to improve:** Start new projects regularly. Try new languages. Breadth of experimentation scores higher than sitting on old repos.

---

### Depth (5 pts)
Specialisation bonus — if you clearly dominate one domain, you earn the depth bonus.

**How to improve:** This rewards focus. If most of your repos cluster around one domain (e.g., AI tooling), you get this automatically.

## Tiers

| Score | Tier | What it means |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 90–100 | 🔵 MASTER | Exceptional breadth, consistency, and impact |
| 75–89 | 🔵 EXPERT | Strong across most dimensions |
| 60–74 | 🟢 CAPABLE | Solid profile with clear strengths |
| 45–59 | 🟠 EMERGING | Active but room to grow |
| 0–44 | 🔴 NASCENT | Early stage — just getting started |

---

## Agent Archetypes

Your archetype is assigned based on your strongest metric combination:

| Archetype | Profile |
|-----------|---------|
| **The Builder** | High consistency + diversity. Ships constantly across domains. |
| **The Strategist** | High quality + impact. Plans well, documents everything, high reach. |
| **The Specialist** | High depth + quality. Goes deep in one domain with precision. |
| **The Explorer** | High diversity + learning. Always trying something new. |
| **The Contributor** | High impact + consistency. Reliable presence, projects people use. |
| **The Newcomer** | Early stage across all dimensions. Everything ahead. |

---

## Behavioural Signals

Beyond the six metrics, the scorecard surfaces four behavioural signals:

- **Velocity** — recent commit rate vs. historical average
- **Maintenance** — ratio of updates to old repos vs. creating new ones
- **Narrative** — how well you explain your work (descriptions, READMEs)
- **Reach** — stars + forks as a proxy for real-world adoption

---

## Leaderboard

- Top 50 scores are shown on the leaderboard
- Ranked by total score
- Click any row to re-score that user with live GitHub data
- Your score is saved automatically — no sign-up required
- Each score gets a unique shareable URL (`?id=UUID`)

---

## Tips for a higher score

1. **Commit daily** — Consistency is worth 25 points, the most of any single metric
2. **Write READMEs** — Quality is 20 points and entirely within your control
3. **Diversify your repos** — Push projects in 3+ languages for full Diversity points
4. **Share your work** — Stars and forks drive Impact; post your repos where people will find them
5. **Keep building** — Learning rewards new projects, not just maintaining old ones

---

## Scoring a repo vs. a user

- **Username** (`crabblack`) → scores the user's entire public profile
- **Repo URL** (`https://github.com/crabblack/my-project`) → scores that specific repository

Repo scores tend to be lower because they reflect a single project, not your full body of work.
